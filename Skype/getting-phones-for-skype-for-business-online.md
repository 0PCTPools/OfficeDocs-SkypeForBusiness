# Getting phones for Skype for Business Online

Skype for Business Online qualifies and support desktop phones for users that want to have a traditional phone experience over using the Skype for Business app. This topic talks about the phones and firmware versions that are supported for use in Skype for Business Online and other information that can help you when you are setting up phones in your organization.
  
    
    

To get the latest updates and most up to date information on supported devices see the  [Skype for Business Device Catalogue](http://partnersolutions.skypeforbusiness.com/solutionscatalog).
## Supported phones

For Skype for Business Online users, you can choose from several models within the  *Certified for Skype for Business Phones*  , and phones running Lync Phone Edition (LPE) listed under Skype for Business Online category in the [Skype for Business Device Catalogue](http://partnersolutions.skypeforbusiness.com/solutionscatalog).
  
    
    
Microsoft is working closely and partnered with Polycom, Yealink, and AudioCodes to develop and certify a wide variety of devices through the Partner IP Phone Program (PIP) for Cloud PBX and Skype for Business Server.
  
    
    
When ordering new phones for Skype for Business, it is important to buy phones with the  *right product ID*  . These product ID's will make sure the phones you receive have the Skype for Business Online qualified version already installed.

For more details on Polycom phones, see  [Voice Solutions for Microsoft](http://www.polycom.com/voice-conferencing-solutions/microsoft-phones.mdl).
  
    
    
For more details on Yealink phones, see  [Skype for Business IP Phones](http://www.yealink.com/product_list.aspx?ProductsCateID=1310&amp;parentcateid=1298&amp;cateid=1310&amp;BaseInfoCateId=1310&amp;Cate_Id=1310&amp;index=2).
  
    
    
For more details on AudioCodes phones, see [ Skype for Business IP Phones](http://www.audiocodes.com/products/skype-for-business-compatible-ip-phone).
  
    
    

> [!NOTE]
> Lync Phone Edition is supported with Skype for Business Online. Mainstream support for LPE platform ends by April/10/2014. , and extended support until April/11/2023 to align with the product support lifecycle of Lync Server 2013. See  [Microsoft Product Lifecycle](https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/lifecycle/search?qid=&amp;alpha=Lync%20Phone%20Edition&amp;Filter=FilterNO) for details on LPE lifecycle. LPE CAP models aren't supported with Skype for Business Online.
  
    
    


## Supported Firmware

This is the minimum software release required for supported phones to work on Cloud PBX:
  
    
    

||||
|:-----|:-----|:-----|
|**Phone type** <br/> |**Minimum firmware** <br/> |**Release Date** <br/> |
|Optimized (Lync Phone Edition)  <br/> |4.0.7577.4463  <br/> |May 2015  <br/> |
|Certified Polycom VVX Series  <br/> |5.4.0A  <br/> |December 2015  <br/> |
|Yealink  <br/> |X.8.1.52  <br/> |February 2017  <br/> |
|AudioCodes  <br/> |3.0.0.459.1  <br/> |December 2016  <br/> |
   

> [!NOTE]
> Lync Phone Edition (LPE) phones you have set up for your on-premises deployment must be updated to the minimum or later required firmware prior to moving those users over to Skype for Business Online. If you move your users from on-premises to Skype for Business Online before you update the firmware on the phones, those phones won't be able to connect to Skype for Business Online. 
  
    
    


## Required Licenses

Skype for Business Online phone doesn't require any additional Microsoft license other than the user licenses. To learn more about the required user licenses See  [Skype for Business add-on licensing](skype-for-business-add-on-licensing.md).
  
    
    
Manufacturer licensing model might vary between open SIP and Skype for Business Certified firmware. If you are repurposing a certified model with an Open SIP firmware, you will need to verify firmware license requirements with the manufacturer.
